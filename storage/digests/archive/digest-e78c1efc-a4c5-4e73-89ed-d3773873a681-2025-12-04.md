## Executive Summary
The Philippine energy sector has witnessed significant developments from November 27 to December 4, 2025, highlighting advancements in renewable energy projects, regulatory approvals, and strategic partnerships aimed at enhancing energy efficiency. Notably, Aseana Holdings Inc. (AHI) has partnered with MPower to implement a Retail Aggregation Program (RAP) that aims to optimize energy procurement for its properties in Aseana City. Furthermore, the National Electrification Administration (NEA) has joined forces with Maharlika Investment Corporation and the Provincial Government of Palawan to improve the island's electrical infrastructure, enhancing reliability and efficiency.

In the realm of renewable energy, Repower Energy Development Corporation (REDC) is expanding its hydroelectric capacity with the acquisition of a 95% stake in the Maramag Hydropower Corporation, which is developing the 25 MW Pulangi IV project. The Energy Regulatory Commission (ERC) has also approved crucial infrastructure projects, including a dedicated 115-kV facility for Prime Solar Solutions Corp. and upgrades for the Davao del Sur Electric Cooperative (DASURECO) to ensure grid safety and reliability.
